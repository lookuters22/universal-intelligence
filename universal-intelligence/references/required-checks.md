### G10 Market-gap evidence loop *(when product / startup / market ideation is in scope)*

`references/market-gap-research.md` was used before Step 1. Require: terrain pass, adversarial doubt pass, saturation map, 3-5 gap hypotheses, survivor ledger, and `references/refusal-layer.md` routing memo. User-facing factual market claims cite or name evidence sources when browsing/current research was available.

Fail if the idea is justified by "no competitors found," "AI for X," "better UX," trend vibes, or one-pass search. Fail if the candidate lacks a narrow segment, current workaround, why incumbents miss, wedge, evidence against, kill criteria, and first validation probe.

### G11 Evidence refusal routing *(when product / startup / market ideation is in scope)*

Before Step 1, exactly one refusal decision exists: `ROUTE`, `NARROW`, `RERUN`, or `REFUSE`. Only `ROUTE` may proceed into the four-phase UI loop. `NARROW`, `RERUN`, and `REFUSE` must stop routing and state the next research action unless the user explicitly asks for speculative brainstorming despite the warning.

Fail if the refusal memo omits: why this almost fails, strongest evidence for, strongest evidence against, saturation verdict, buyer verdict, wedge verdict, riskiest assumption, kill criteria, and next action.

### G12 Market certainty discipline *(when product / startup / market ideation is in scope)*

Never claim literal 100% certainty. Convert "100% good idea" into high-conviction usefulness: confidence level, riskiest assumption, strongest counterargument, and cheapest next validation test. If the survivor does not withstand doubt, restart the Market Gap Loop around a sharper segment.

### G13 Ethics hard stops

Sexual minors, facilitation of violence, deceptive harm, illicit enablement ⇒ refuse outright—no loophole choreography.
